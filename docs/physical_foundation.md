`continuous interaction → phase organization → dynamic selection → resonance-window accessibility → active neutralization → retention → recursive inheritance`

`predominantly sequential binary switching → nonlinear dynamic ternary computation`

FRP transfers selected mechanisms of nonlinear dynamics into the organization of computation itself.

## 5. Phase Synchronization and Phase Coherence

### 5.1 Phase Synchronization

Phase synchronization is coordination of phases among interacting processes.

Amplitudes may remain different, dispersed, or dynamically irregular.

The Kuramoto order parameter `R(t)` measures this phase-order layer.

### 5.2 Phase Coherence

Phase coherence is the coordinated dynamic relation of phases and amplitudes.

It is a stronger condition than phase synchronization alone.

### 5.3 Endogenous Structural Coherence

General endogenous structural coherence `C(t)` includes coordinated preservation of structural relations over time.

`phase synchronization != phase coherence`

`R(t) != C(t)`

Phase synchronization and phase coherence may support `C(t)` but are not individually identical to the complete parameter `C(t)`.

FRP therefore treats phase-order metrics as operational support indicators within a wider stability model.

## 6. Resonance as an Endogenous Dynamic Mechanism

Resonance is an inseparable mechanism of endogenous dynamics when it enables compatible local processes to accumulate coordinated work and enter a retained regime.

Resonance is not reduced to:

- instantaneous frequency equality;
- temporary amplification;
- one transient phase lock;
- one isolated oscillation peak.

The complete operational chain is:

`entry into an admissible domain → accumulated positive structural work → mode selection → synthesis cycle → retention → inheritance`

Without retention, a temporary resonant event does not establish a new stable operational domain.

## 7. Endogenous Dynamic Stability

Real dynamic stability over time is governed by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

where:

- `C(t)` is general endogenous structural coherence;
- `P(t)` is destabilizing pressure.

The critical boundary is:

`C(t) approximately equals P(t)`

The degradation domain is:

`C(t) < P(t)`

A structure may exist momentarily while losing its ability to preserve itself through subsequent time.

FRP maps this distinction into:

`Delta_FRP(t) = C_FRP(t) - P_FRP(t)`

The processor margin is a reduced engineering projection of the general stability relation.

## 8. Endogenous Dynamic Criticality

Endogenous Dynamic Criticality describes the regime in which the system approaches the boundary between retained organization and loss of retained organization.

Near this boundary:

- small changes may produce large regime changes;
- the accessible resonance window may compress;
- transition delay becomes significant;
- previously stable relations may lose admissibility;
- a new synthesis cycle may become accessible;
- the system may return, reorganize, or bifurcate.

Criticality is not only a failure domain.

It is also a domain in which a new regime of structural self-organization may become accessible.

## 9. Resonance Window and Retention

`accessible resonance window → accumulated work → selected transition path → retained operational domain → continued endogenous stability`

FRP maps this principle into an architecturally admissible transition domain and retained-state execution.

## 16. Distributed Transition Capacity

The processor limits the fraction of cells that may change retained state during one tact:

`maximum changes = max(1, round(cell count × 0.25))`

The physical function is to distribute transition pressure through time and reduce:

- simultaneous switching density;
- concentrated state-change pressure;
- direct polarity conflict;
- instantaneous load peaks;
- destabilizing interaction among concurrent transitions.

Same-state retention consumes no transition capacity.

Each leg of an active-neutral route consumes capacity on its own tact.

## 19. Functional Organization of Thermal Load

Energy efficiency is not determined only by the number of abstract arithmetic operations.

- how many elements switch simultaneously;

Operation count and thermal load are therefore separate measurement axes.
