# Global Coherent Synthesis Topology

This document describes the global coherent synthesis topology used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

Within the FPR framework, distributed synchronization structures may recursively integrate into large-scale coherent synthesis topology.

Global coherent synthesis represents emergence of unified resonance-retention organization across interacting synchronization domains.

Global coherent synthesis emerges through:

- recursive synchronization interaction,
- resonance-domain integration,
- harmonic coherence propagation,
- adaptive resonance restructuring,
- and nonlinear coherent-retention stabilization.

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of global coherent synthesis evolution.

Stable global synthesis topology may support:

- large-scale synchronization persistence,
- distributed coherence continuity,

Stable synthesis progressively increases large-scale coherence-retention capability across distributed resonance structures.

- metastable synchronization-integration states,

## 5. Recursive Global Reinforcement

Stable global synthesis structures recursively reinforce:

- adaptive synchronization pathways,
- resonance-domain continuity,

- recursive coherence inheritance,

Recursive reinforcement progressively increases large-scale synchronization stability.

## 6. Global Synthesis Destabilization

Unstable global synthesis may amplify:

- synchronization divergence,
- incoherent resonance propagation,
- recursive fragmentation cascades,
- metastable overshoot dynamics,
- and coherent-retention degradation.

The processor therefore continuously attempts to balance structural synthesis expansion and synchronization stability.

Stable global coherent synthesis remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherent-retention pathways.
