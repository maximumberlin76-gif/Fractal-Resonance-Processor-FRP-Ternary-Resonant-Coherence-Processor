# Coherent Attractor Formation

This document describes the coherent-attractor formation mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Attractor Formation Principle

Within the FPR framework, stable coherent computational regimes emerge through recursive phase synchronization and sustained coherence accumulation.

A coherent attractor represents a dynamically stabilized phase configuration capable of retaining synchronization stability over time.

## 2. Coherence Accumulation Dynamics

Attractor formation requires sufficient accumulation of:

- phase synchronization stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- coherence-retention capability,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and recursive stabilization reinforcement.

The attractor progressively stabilizes as coherent interaction persists.

## 3. Transitional Attractor States

The framework distinguishes transitional attractor states from fully stabilized coherent attractors.

Transitional attractor states may exhibit:

- unstable phase-lock behavior,
- fluctuating synchronization regions,
- partial coherence retention,
- and dynamically evolving resonance compatibility.

These states may either stabilize into coherent attractors or collapse into dissipative fragmentation.

## 4. Recursive Attractor Reinforcement

Coherent attractors reinforce neighboring synchronization structures recursively.

This recursive reinforcement may increase:

- local coherence stability,
- regional synchronization persistence,
- resonance-retention capability,
- and large-scale coherence propagation.

## 5. Dissipative Attractor Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherence accumulation, coherent attractors progressively destabilize.

This may produce:

- synchronization decay,
- coherence fragmentation,
- unstable phase drift,
- and collapse of coherent retention structures.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent attractor retention remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

within interacting synchronization regions.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent attractor formation is interpreted as a recursive resonance-driven phase-stabilization process operating through sustained synchronization dynamics and coherence-retention accumulation.
