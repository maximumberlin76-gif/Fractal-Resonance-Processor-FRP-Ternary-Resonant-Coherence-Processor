# Recursive Coherence Compression

This document describes the recursive coherence-compression mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Compression Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent computational structures may be recursively compressed without complete loss of phase-coherence organization.

Compression is interpreted not as simple symbolic data reduction alone, but as preservation of dynamically significant coherent phase relationships.

## 2. Coherent Compression Dynamics

Recursive coherence compression operates through:

- resonance-compatible phase reduction,
- coherent structure retention,
- synchronization-pattern preservation,
- and recursive stabilization of compressed phase relations.

The processor attempts to preserve the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase synchronization during compression.

## 3. Selective Compression Behavior

The processor selectively compresses:

- stable synchronization structures,
- resonance-compatible phase configurations,
- coherent attractor patterns,
- and recursively stabilized coherence domains.

Highly dissipative or incoherent structures are progressively discarded through fragmentation filtering.

## 4. Recursive Compression Hierarchy

Compression operates recursively across interacting synchronization scales.

Compressed coherent structures may retain:

- synchronization persistence,
- resonance compatibility,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and coherent-retention capability.

Recursive compression therefore preserves dynamically important coherence topology rather than isolated symbolic states alone.

## 5. Dissipative Compression Failure

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherent-retention capability during compression, stable phase organization progressively collapses.

This may produce:

- synchronization degradation,
- coherence fragmentation,
- unstable phase divergence,
- and loss of retained coherent structure.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable recursive coherence compression remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and coherent-retention structures.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, recursive coherence compression is interpreted as a resonance-driven phase-coherence retention mechanism operating through nonlinear synchronization stabilization and recursive coherent-structure preservation dynamics.
