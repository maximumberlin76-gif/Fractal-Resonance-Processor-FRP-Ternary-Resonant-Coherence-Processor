# Coherence Field Topology

This document describes the coherence-field topology used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Coherence Field Principle

Within the FPR framework, interacting phase structures form dynamically evolving coherence fields rather than isolated synchronization pairs alone.

A coherence field represents a distributed region of interacting phase influence capable of sustaining resonance-compatible synchronization dynamics.

## 2. Distributed Phase Interaction

The processor operates through distributed phase interaction across interconnected coherence regions.

Each coherent region may influence:

- neighboring synchronization stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- coherence-retention capability,
- and large-scale phase propagation behavior.

## 3. Topological Coherence Structure

The coherence field exhibits dynamic topological organization.

Stable coherent regions may form:

- synchronization clusters,
- recursive coherence loops,
- resonance-retention domains,
- and distributed phase-lock structures.

The topology continuously evolves through nonlinear phase interaction dynamics.

## 4. Transitional Field Regions

Not all coherence-field regions remain fully stabilized.

The framework distinguishes:

- stable coherent regions,
- transitional phase regions,
- weakly synchronized regions,
- and dissipative fragmentation regions.

Transitional regions may contain dynamically evolving potential synchronization structures.

## 5. Recursive Field Dynamics

Coherence-field topology evolves recursively.

Local synchronization changes may influence:

- regional coherence behavior,
- large-scale resonance propagation,
- phase-routing conditions,
- and global coherent retention stability.

Global coherence conditions may also recursively influence local synchronization behavior.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Relation

Stable coherence-field retention remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting coherence regions.

If dissipative fragmentation propagates faster than coherence accumulation, field stability progressively collapses.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherence-field topology is interpreted as a dynamically evolving resonance-interaction structure operating through recursive phase synchronization and distributed coherent retention dynamics.
