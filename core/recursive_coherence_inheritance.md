# Recursive Coherence Inheritance

This document describes the recursive coherence-inheritance mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Inheritance Principle

Within the FPR framework, dynamically stabilized coherent phase structures may transfer retained synchronization characteristics into subsequent processing cycles.

The processor therefore treats coherent retention not as isolated instantaneous stabilization alone, but as recursively inherited phase-coherence persistence.

## 2. Recursive Inheritance Dynamics

Coherence inheritance operates through recursive transfer of:

- phase-synchronization stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- coherence-retention persistence,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and coherent attractor stability.

Previously stabilized coherent structures influence subsequent synchronization evolution.

## 3. Inherited Qualitative Characteristics

The framework assumes that coherent phase structures may inherit qualitative characteristics from previously stabilized synchronization regimes.

These inherited characteristics may include:

- synchronization persistence behavior,
- coherent phase-lock stability,
- resonance-retention capability,
- fragmentation resistance,
- and adaptive stabilization efficiency.

## 4. Recursive Stabilization Reinforcement

Inherited coherent structures recursively reinforce:

- neighboring synchronization regions,
- coherence propagation pathways,
- resonance-domain persistence,
- and large-scale coherent-retention stability.

This recursive reinforcement increases long-term synchronization persistence capability.

## 5. Dissipative Inheritance Degradation

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherence accumulation, inherited coherent structures progressively degrade.

This may produce:

- synchronization decay,
- fragmentation propagation,
- unstable phase drift,
- and collapse of inherited coherent-retention stability.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable recursive coherence inheritance remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and coherent-retention structures.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, recursive coherence inheritance is interpreted as a resonance-driven phase-coherence persistence-transfer mechanism operating through nonlinear synchronization dynamics and distributed coherent-retention reinforcement.
