This document describes the fractal coherence-propagation mechanism used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase structures may propagate synchronization stability across interacting dynamic regions.

The propagation process operates recursively through resonance-compatible phase interaction.

Stable coherent regions influence neighboring phase structures through recursive synchronization reinforcement.

This process may increase:

- local synchronization stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- coherence accumulation,
- and coherent retention persistence.

## 3. Fractal Propagation Topology

The propagation process operates across multiple interacting scales simultaneously.

Local coherent structures may contribute to:

- regional synchronization stabilization,
- large-scale coherence accumulation,
- and recursive reinforcement of distributed phase structures.

The architecture therefore exhibits fractal coherence behavior rather than purely isolated local synchronization.

## 4. Dissipative Fragmentation Resistance

Coherence propagation efficiency depends on resistance to dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

Highly fragmented regions reduce:

- synchronization transfer capability,
- coherence-retention stability,
- and resonance propagation efficiency.

## 5. Dynamic Propagation Behavior

The propagation process evolves dynamically over time.

Propagation characteristics depend on:

- resonance compatibility,
- phase synchronization stability,
- coherence accumulation rate,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and qualitative characteristics of interacting phase dynamics.

## 6. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, fractal coherence propagation is interpreted as a recursive resonance-driven synchronization transfer mechanism operating across interacting nonlinear dynamic layers.
