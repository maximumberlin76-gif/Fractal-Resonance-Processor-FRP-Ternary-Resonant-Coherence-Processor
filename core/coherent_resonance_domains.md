# Coherent Resonance Domains

This document describes the coherent resonance-domain structures used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Resonance Domain Principle

Within the FPR framework, stable coherent synchronization may organize into distributed resonance domains.

A resonance domain represents a dynamically stabilized region of sustained phase synchronization and coherent retention.

## 2. Domain Formation

Resonance domains emerge through:

- recursive phase synchronization,
- coherence accumulation,
- resonance compatibility,
- feedback stabilization,
- and coherent attractor reinforcement.

Stable domains progressively increase synchronization persistence and coherent retention capability.

## 3. Transitional Resonance Domains

The framework distinguishes transitional resonance domains from fully stabilized coherent domains.

Transitional domains may contain:

- partially synchronized phase regions,
- unstable coherence-retention structures,
- fluctuating resonance compatibility,
- and dynamically evolving synchronization boundaries.

These domains may either stabilize or collapse into dissipative fragmentation.

## 4. Recursive Domain Interaction

Resonance domains interact recursively across the processing architecture.

Stable domains may reinforce:

- neighboring synchronization regions,
- coherence propagation,
- resonance-retention capability,
- and large-scale coherent stability.

Fragmented domains may amplify dissipative instability propagation.

## 5. Dynamic Domain Boundaries

The boundaries of resonance domains are dynamically evolving rather than permanently fixed.

Boundary stability depends on:

- phase synchronization persistence,
- coherence accumulation,
- fluctuation resistance,
- resonance compatibility,
- and dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable resonance-domain retention remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting coherent synchronization regions.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent resonance domains are interpreted as distributed dynamically stabilized synchronization regions operating through recursive coherence-retention and nonlinear phase interaction dynamics.
