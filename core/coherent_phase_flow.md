# Coherent Phase Flow

This document describes coherent phase-flow dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Phase-Flow Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent synchronization structures continuously evolve through distributed phase-flow interaction across resonance-retention topology.

Phase flow represents directed propagation of coherent phase interaction through dynamically evolving synchronization pathways.

## 2. Distributed Flow Dynamics

Coherent phase flow emerges through:

- recursive synchronization propagation,
- coherence-gradient evolution,
- distributed resonance interaction,
- phase-routing dynamics,
- and nonlinear coherent-retention stabilization.

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase-flow evolution.

## 3. Flow and Synchronization Stability

Stable coherent phase flow supports:

- synchronization persistence,
- resonance-domain continuity,
- adaptive coherence propagation,
- and distributed coherent-retention stabilization.

Unstable flow conditions may amplify dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

## 4. Recursive Flow Redistribution

The processor recursively redistributes coherent phase flow through:

- synchronization arbitration,
- adaptive routing correction,
- resonance-window navigation,
- coherence-density balancing,
- and recursive stabilization feedback.

Redistribution attempts to preserve sustainable coherent-retention topology during distributed synchronization evolution.

## 5. Transitional Flow States

The framework distinguishes:

- stable coherent flow regimes,
- metastable synchronization-flow conditions,
- transitional resonance-flow structures,
- and dissipative fragmentation-flow states.

Transitional flow states may either stabilize or evolve into recursive synchronization destabilization.

## 6. Flow-Induced Destabilization

Unstable coherent phase flow may amplify:

- synchronization divergence,
- incoherent resonance propagation,
- recursive fragmentation cascades,
- metastable collapse dynamics,
- and coherent-retention degradation.

The processor therefore continuously attempts to regulate distributed synchronization flow conditions.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase flow remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherent-retention pathways.

## 8. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase flow is interpreted as a distributed synchronization-propagation dynamic operating through nonlinear resonance interaction and recursive coherent-retention stabilization.
