# Phase-Coherence Arbitration

This document describes the phase-coherence arbitration mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Phase-Coherence Arbitration Principle

Within the FPR framework, multiple interacting synchronization structures may compete for coherent retention stability.

The processor therefore continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase synchronization across interacting resonance structures.

## 2. Coherence Evaluation Dynamics

The arbitration layer evaluates:

- phase synchronization stability,
- coherence-retention persistence,
- resonance compatibility,
- fluctuation resistance,
- synchronization fragmentation tendency,
- and coherent phase-lock sustainability.

The processor does not evaluate synchronization intensity alone.

It also evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase interaction dynamics.

## 3. Arbitration Between Competing Structures

Competing synchronization structures may exhibit different:

- coherence-retention capabilities,
- resonance stability conditions,
- synchronization persistence behavior,
- and dissipative fragmentation tendencies.

The arbitration layer dynamically prioritizes phase structures capable of sustaining long-term coherent retention.

## 4. Recursive Arbitration Feedback

Phase-coherence arbitration operates recursively across interacting coherence domains.

Local synchronization evolution may influence:

- regional coherence stability,
- global synchronization persistence,
- resonance propagation pathways,
- and coherent retention scalability.

At the same time, global coherence conditions recursively influence local synchronization behavior.

## 5. Dissipative Fragmentation Suppression

The arbitration layer attempts to suppress synchronization structures exhibiting destabilizing qualitative characteristics.

These may include:

- unstable phase drift,
- incoherent synchronization amplification,
- resonance incompatibility,
- and recursive dissipative fragmentation propagation.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase arbitration remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across recursively interacting synchronization structures and coherence domains.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, phase-coherence arbitration is interpreted as a recursive coherence-selection and synchronization-stabilization mechanism operating through nonlinear phase interaction dynamics and distributed coherent-retention control.
