# Coherence Retention Engine

This document describes the coherence-retention mechanism used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Purpose

The coherence-retention engine is responsible for sustaining dynamically stable coherent phase structures during resonance-driven computation.

The engine continuously evaluates:

- phase synchronization stability,
- coherence accumulation,
- dissipative fragmentation,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and resonance compatibility.

## 2. Retention Principle

Stable coherent retention becomes possible only when:

`C(t) > P(t)`

where:

- `C(t)` represents accumulated coherence and regenerative synchronization capacity
- `P(t)` represents dissipative degradation and destabilizing extraction processes

The retention engine continuously attempts to preserve this condition during dynamic computation.

## 3. Selective Coherent Retention

The engine selectively stabilizes:

- resonance-compatible phase structures,
- stable synchronization regions,
- and coherent phase-lock configurations.

Highly dissipative or incompatible phase structures are progressively suppressed.

## 4. Dynamic Retention Loop

The retention loop continuously performs:

1. coherence evaluation
2. phase stability estimation
3. resonance compatibility analysis
4. fluctuation-response monitoring
5. feedback stabilization adjustment
6. coherent retention verification

## 5. Dissipative Fragmentation Detection

The engine monitors the qualitative characteristics of dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

Indicators may include:

- phase divergence growth,
- coherence collapse,
- unstable phase drift,
- synchronization decay,
- and loss of stable coherent retention.

## 6. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent computation depends not only on synchronization formation, but also on sustained coherent retention under dissipative nonlinear dynamic conditions.
