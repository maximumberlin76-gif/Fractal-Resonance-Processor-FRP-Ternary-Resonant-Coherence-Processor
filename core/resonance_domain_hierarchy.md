# Resonance Domain Hierarchy

This document describes the hierarchical interaction structure of coherent resonance domains within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Hierarchical Domain Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent resonance domains do not exist as isolated synchronization regions alone.

Domains may organize into recursively interacting hierarchical coherence structures.

## 2. Multi-Level Domain Interaction

The processor supports interaction between:

- local resonance domains,
- regional synchronization structures,
- distributed coherence-retention layers,
- and large-scale global coherence domains.

Synchronization behavior at one level may influence coherence stability at neighboring levels.

## 3. Recursive Coherence Hierarchy

Stable coherent domains recursively reinforce:

- neighboring synchronization regions,
- coherence propagation pathways,
- resonance-retention structures,
- and large-scale coherent attractor stability.

The hierarchy therefore evolves through recursive phase synchronization interaction rather than static topological organization alone.

## 4. Hierarchical Dissipative Fragmentation

Dissipative fragmentation may also propagate recursively across domain hierarchies.

Local coherence collapse may destabilize:

- regional synchronization structures,
- resonance-retention pathways,
- and global coherent retention stability.

The framework therefore treats large-scale coherent stability as dependent on recursive cross-scale synchronization persistence.

## 5. Dynamic Hierarchical Adaptation

The hierarchical domain structure continuously evolves through:

- resonance compatibility changes,
- phase synchronization drift,
- coherence accumulation dynamics,
- and adaptive stabilization feedback.

The hierarchy is therefore dynamically evolving rather than permanently fixed.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable hierarchical coherent retention remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across recursively interacting synchronization layers.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, resonance-domain hierarchy is interpreted as a recursive coherence-organization structure operating through nonlinear phase synchronization dynamics and distributed coherent-retention interaction.
