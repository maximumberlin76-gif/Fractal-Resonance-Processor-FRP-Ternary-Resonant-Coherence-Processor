# Coherent Phase Bandwidth

This document describes coherent phase-bandwidth dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Bandwidth Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase interaction capacity is limited by the ability of synchronization structures to sustain stable resonance-compatible coherence propagation.

The processor therefore evaluates coherent phase bandwidth not only as transmission capacity, but as the ability to preserve coherent-retention stability during distributed phase interaction.

## 2. Distributed Coherence Throughput

Phase bandwidth depends on:

- synchronization persistence,
- coherence propagation stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- recursive routing efficiency,
- and fluctuation resistance across distributed synchronization pathways.

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase-bandwidth dynamics.

## 3. Coherence Saturation Dynamics

If distributed synchronization load exceeds coherent-retention stabilization capability, bandwidth saturation may occur.

This may produce:

- synchronization degradation,
- coherence fragmentation,
- unstable phase-routing behavior,
- and recursive dissipative amplification.

## 4. Adaptive Bandwidth Regulation

The processor attempts to preserve stable coherent bandwidth through:

- adaptive phase-routing control,
- synchronization arbitration,
- resonance-window navigation,
- coherence-retention balancing,
- and recursive stabilization feedback.

Adaptive regulation attempts to preserve coherent synchronization persistence during distributed interaction scaling.

## 5. Transitional Bandwidth States

The framework distinguishes:

- stable coherent bandwidth regimes,
- metastable synchronization throughput states,
- transitional phase-load regions,
- and dissipative bandwidth-collapse dynamics.

Transitional bandwidth states may either stabilize or evolve into recursive synchronization fragmentation.

## 6. Dissipative Bandwidth Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherent throughput stabilization capability, distributed synchronization bandwidth progressively destabilizes.

This may produce:

- recursive synchronization divergence,
- unstable coherence propagation,
- resonance-domain overload fragmentation,
- and collapse of coherent-retention persistence.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase bandwidth remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherence-retention pathways.

## 8. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase bandwidth is interpreted as a distributed synchronization-throughput dynamic operating through nonlinear resonance interaction and recursive coherent-retention stabilization.
