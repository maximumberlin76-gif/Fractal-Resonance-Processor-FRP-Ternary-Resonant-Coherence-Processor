# Coherent Phase Gradient

This document describes coherent phase-gradient dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Phase-Gradient Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent synchronization propagation is influenced by distributed phase-coherence gradients across interacting resonance structures.

A phase gradient represents directional variation in:

- coherence accumulation,
- synchronization persistence,
- resonance compatibility,
- and coherent-retention stability.

## 2. Distributed Gradient Dynamics

Phase gradients emerge through:

- recursive synchronization interaction,
- distributed coherence propagation,
- resonance-domain topology,
- fluctuation-driven phase variation,
- and dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase-gradient evolution.

## 3. Gradient-Driven Synchronization Propagation

Synchronization propagation tends to evolve toward regions exhibiting:

- higher coherence-retention stability,
- stronger resonance compatibility,
- lower dissipative fragmentation,
- and increased synchronization persistence.

Phase gradients therefore influence resonance-routing behavior across distributed coherence structures.

## 4. Recursive Gradient Reinforcement

Stable coherent gradients recursively reinforce:

- synchronization propagation pathways,
- resonance-domain connectivity,
- coherence-retention persistence,
- and distributed phase-stabilization behavior.

Recursive reinforcement progressively increases coherent propagation stability.

## 5. Gradient Destabilization Dynamics

Unstable phase gradients may amplify:

- synchronization divergence,
- incoherent routing propagation,
- metastable fragmentation cascades,
- and dissipative phase drift.

The processor therefore attempts to stabilize dynamically evolving coherence gradients during distributed synchronization interaction.

## 6. Adaptive Gradient Regulation

The processor adaptively regulates phase gradients through:

- synchronization arbitration,
- phase-routing adjustment,
- resonance-window navigation,
- coherence-density balancing,
- and recursive stabilization feedback.

Adaptive regulation attempts to preserve stable distributed coherence propagation topology.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase gradients remain possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherence-retention pathways.

## 8. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase gradients are interpreted as directional synchronization-propagation dynamics operating through nonlinear resonance interaction and recursive coherent-retention stabilization.
