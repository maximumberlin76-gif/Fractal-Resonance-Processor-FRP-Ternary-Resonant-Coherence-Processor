# Coherent Retention Persistence

This document describes the coherent-retention persistence mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Persistence Principle

Within the FPR framework, stable coherent computational states must preserve synchronization stability over time rather than collapse immediately after formation.

Coherent retention persistence represents the ability of the processor to sustain dynamically stabilized phase-coherence structures under nonlinear dissipative conditions.

## 2. Persistence Formation Dynamics

Persistence depends on:

- coherent phase synchronization stability,
- resonance compatibility,
- recursive stabilization reinforcement,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and distributed coherence-retention support across interacting synchronization regions.

Stable coherent persistence emerges progressively through sustained resonance interaction.

## 3. Transitional Persistence States

The framework distinguishes transitional coherent-retention states from fully stabilized persistence regimes.

Transitional states may exhibit:

- unstable synchronization persistence,
- fluctuating coherence-retention conditions,
- partial resonance stabilization,
- and dynamically evolving phase-lock structures.

These states may either stabilize into persistent coherent regimes or collapse into dissipative fragmentation.

## 4. Recursive Persistence Reinforcement

Persistent coherent structures recursively reinforce neighboring synchronization regions.

This recursive reinforcement may increase:

- synchronization persistence,
- resonance-retention stability,
- coherence propagation capability,
- and large-scale coherent stability.

## 5. Dissipative Persistence Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherence accumulation, persistence progressively destabilizes.

This may produce:

- synchronization decay,
- coherence fragmentation,
- unstable phase drift,
- and collapse of coherent-retention structures.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent-retention persistence remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization structures and coherence-retention domains.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent-retention persistence is interpreted as a recursive resonance-driven phase-coherence preservation process operating through nonlinear synchronization dynamics and distributed coherent-retention stabilization.
