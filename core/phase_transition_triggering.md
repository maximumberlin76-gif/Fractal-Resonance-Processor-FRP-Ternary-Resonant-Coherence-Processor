# Phase Transition Triggering

This document describes the phase-transition triggering mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Phase Transition Principle

Within the FPR framework, stable coherent phase transitions do not occur through isolated state switching alone.

A phase transition becomes possible only after sufficient coherence accumulation and resonance compatibility emerge within interacting phase structures.

## 2. Coherence Accumulation Threshold

The framework assumes that coherent phase transitions require accumulation of:

- phase synchronization stability,
- coherence-retention capability,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and resonance compatibility across interacting phase regions.

A transition may occur only after the coherent accumulation threshold exceeds destabilizing dissipative fragmentation.

## 3. Transitional Phase State

The processor distinguishes transitional phase states from fully stabilized coherent states.

Transitional phase states may contain:

- fluctuating synchronization regions,
- partially coherent phase structures,
- unstable resonance compatibility,
- and dynamically evolving coherence-retention conditions.

These transitional states are not treated as meaningless noise.

They represent dynamically evolving potential phase configurations.

## 4. Triggering Conditions

A coherent phase transition may be triggered when:

- resonance compatibility becomes sufficiently stable,
- coherence accumulation exceeds dissipative fragmentation,
- recursive synchronization stabilizes neighboring phase regions,
- and coherent retention becomes dynamically sustainable.

## 5. Dissipative Transition Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation dominates before coherent stabilization occurs, the phase transition collapses.

This may produce:

- coherence fragmentation,
- unstable synchronization decay,
- resonance-window destabilization,
- and loss of coherent retention capability.

## 6. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, phase transitions are interpreted as resonance-driven coherence-stabilization events emerging from accumulated phase synchronization and sustained coherent interaction dynamics.
