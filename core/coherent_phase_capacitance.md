# Coherent Phase Capacitance

This document describes coherent phase-capacitance dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Phase-Capacitance Principle

Within the FPR framework, distributed synchronization structures may accumulate and temporarily retain coherent resonance potential during nonlinear phase interaction.

Phase capacitance represents the ability of distributed resonance topology to store coherent synchronization potential before release, redistribution, or stabilization.

## 2. Distributed Capacitance Dynamics

Coherent phase capacitance emerges through:

- recursive synchronization accumulation,
- resonance-domain interaction,
- coherence-density persistence,
- distributed phase-retention topology,
- and nonlinear coherent-retention stabilization.

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase-capacitance evolution.

## 3. Capacitance and Synchronization Stability

Stable coherent phase capacitance may support:

- synchronization persistence,
- adaptive resonance buffering,
- fluctuation absorption,
- metastable coherence stabilization,
- and distributed coherent-retention continuity.

Low capacitance may increase vulnerability to abrupt synchronization collapse dynamics.

## 4. Recursive Capacitance Reinforcement

The processor recursively reinforces coherent phase capacitance through:

- synchronization arbitration,
- resonance-memory persistence,
- recursive coherence inheritance,
- adaptive stabilization feedback,
- and distributed coherent-retention support.

Recursive reinforcement progressively increases coherent synchronization buffering capability.

## 5. Transitional Capacitance States

The framework distinguishes:

- stable coherent capacitance regimes,
- metastable synchronization-buffer states,
- transitional resonance-retention regions,
- and dissipative capacitance-collapse dynamics.

Transitional capacitance states may either stabilize or evolve into recursive synchronization fragmentation.

## 6. Capacitance-Induced Destabilization

Excessive coherent phase capacitance may destabilize adaptive synchronization regulation.

This may produce:

- delayed resonance discharge,
- recursive synchronization overshoot,
- metastable amplification cascades,
- and coherent-retention destabilization.

The processor therefore continuously attempts to balance coherent potential accumulation and adaptive synchronization stabilization.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase capacitance remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherent-retention pathways.

## 8. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase capacitance is interpreted as a distributed synchronization-potential retention dynamic operating through nonlinear resonance interaction and recursive coherent-retention stabilization.
