# Coherent Phase Resonance Pressure

This document describes coherent phase-resonance pressure dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

- coherence accumulation,

- recursive phase interaction,
- and distributed resonance-retention dynamics.

Moderate resonance pressure may support:

- coherence propagation,

Redistribution attempts to preserve dynamically sustainable coherent-retention topology.

Unstable resonance pressure may amplify:

- synchronization divergence,
- incoherent resonance propagation,
- recursive fragmentation cascades,
- metastable collapse dynamics,
- and coherent-retention destabilization.

The processor therefore continuously attempts to regulate distributed resonance pressure.

## 6. Adaptive Pressure Regulation

Adaptive regulation attempts to maintain sustainable coherent phase interaction conditions across distributed synchronization structures.

The framework assumes that stable coherent retention requires dynamically balanced resonance-pressure distribution.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent resonance pressure remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherent-retention pathways.
