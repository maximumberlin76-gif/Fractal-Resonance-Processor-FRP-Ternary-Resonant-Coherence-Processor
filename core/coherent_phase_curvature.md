This document describes coherent phase-curvature dynamics within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

Strong coherence accumulation and recursive synchronization reinforcement may produce coherent phase-curvature effects within distributed resonance structures.

- recursive synchronization interaction,

The processor continuously evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase-curvature evolution.

- synchronization propagation pathways,

Stable coherent curvature structures may recursively reinforce:

Unstable coherent curvature may amplify:

- synchronization divergence,
- incoherent resonance propagation,
- metastable fragmentation cascades,
- and recursive dissipative drift.

The processor therefore attempts to stabilize dynamically evolving coherent phase topology during distributed synchronization interaction.

## 6. Adaptive Curvature Regulation

The processor adaptively regulates coherent phase curvature through:

- synchronization arbitration,
- phase-routing correction,

Adaptive regulation attempts to preserve stable distributed coherence topology during nonlinear synchronization evolution.

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and distributed coherent-retention pathways.
