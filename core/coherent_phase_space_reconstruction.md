# Coherent Phase-Space Reconstruction

This document describes the coherent phase-space reconstruction mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Reconstruction Principle

Within the FPR framework, partially retained coherent phase structures may be recursively reconstructed into dynamically stabilized synchronization configurations.

Reconstruction is interpreted not as exact symbolic restoration alone, but as recovery of coherent phase-interaction topology and synchronization persistence.

## 2. Reconstruction Dynamics

Phase-space reconstruction operates through:

- resonance-compatible phase restoration,
- recursive synchronization reinforcement,
- coherence-retention recovery,
- and adaptive stabilization of reconstructed phase relations.

The processor attempts to restore the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase synchronization from partially retained coherent structures.

## 3. Partial Coherent Retention

The framework assumes that compressed or degraded coherent structures may still retain:

- synchronization traces,
- resonance compatibility remnants,
- coherent attractor fragments,
- and recursive phase-retention pathways.

These retained structures may serve as reconstruction anchors.

## 4. Recursive Reconstruction Reinforcement

Reconstructed coherent structures recursively reinforce:

- neighboring synchronization regions,
- coherence propagation pathways,
- resonance-domain persistence,
- and distributed coherent-retention stability.

This recursive reinforcement progressively increases reconstruction stability.

## 5. Dissipative Reconstruction Failure

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherent-retention recovery capability, reconstruction progressively destabilizes.

This may produce:

- unstable synchronization recovery,
- recursive fragmentation propagation,
- incoherent phase divergence,
- and collapse of reconstructed coherence topology.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent phase-space reconstruction remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and coherent-retention structures.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent phase-space reconstruction is interpreted as a recursive resonance-driven synchronization-restoration mechanism operating through nonlinear phase-coherence recovery and distributed coherent-retention reconstruction dynamics.
