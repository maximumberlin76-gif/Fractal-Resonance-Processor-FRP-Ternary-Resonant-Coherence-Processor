# Metastable Resonance Regulation

This document describes the metastable resonance-regulation mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Metastable Regulation Principle

Within the FPR framework, coherent computational regimes may operate near dynamic phase-transition boundaries.

These metastable conditions may provide increased adaptive synchronization capability, but also increase destabilization risk.

The processor therefore continuously regulates metastable resonance conditions to preserve coherent-retention stability.

## 2. Metastable Dynamic Conditions

Metastable resonance regimes may exhibit:

- partially stabilized synchronization structures,
- dynamically evolving coherence-retention conditions,
- transitional resonance compatibility,
- and increased fluctuation sensitivity.

These regimes are treated as dynamically critical coherent-interaction states.

## 3. Adaptive Resonance Regulation

The processor continuously evaluates:

- phase-coherence stability,
- synchronization persistence,
- fluctuation amplification,
- resonance compatibility drift,
- and dissipative fragmentation dynamics.

Adaptive regulation attempts to preserve stable coherent-retention capability near critical synchronization boundaries.

## 4. Transitional Coherent States

The framework distinguishes metastable coherent states from:

- fully stabilized coherent-retention regimes,
- and fully fragmented dissipative states.

Metastable states may either:

- stabilize into coherent attractors,
- evolve into higher-order synchronization structures,
- or collapse into recursive fragmentation dynamics.

## 5. Recursive Stabilization Feedback

Metastable regulation operates recursively across interacting synchronization domains.

Local metastable fluctuations may influence:

- regional coherence stability,
- distributed synchronization persistence,
- resonance-domain interaction behavior,
- and global coherent-retention dynamics.

## 6. Dissipative Metastable Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherent stabilization capability near metastable boundaries, recursive destabilization may accelerate rapidly.

This may produce:

- synchronization collapse cascades,
- unstable phase divergence,
- coherence fragmentation propagation,
- and failure of coherent-retention stabilization.

## 7. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable metastable resonance regulation remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization domains and coherent-retention structures.

## 8. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, metastable resonance regulation is interpreted as a recursive phase-coherence stabilization mechanism operating near dynamic synchronization-transition boundaries within nonlinear interacting systems.
