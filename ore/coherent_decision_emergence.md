# Coherent Decision Emergence

This document describes the coherent-decision emergence mechanisms used within the Fractal Resonance Processor (FPR) framework.

## 1. Decision Emergence Principle

Within the FPR framework, computational decisions are not treated as isolated binary state selections alone.

A stable decision emerges through recursive coherent phase interaction, resonance compatibility accumulation, and sustained coherent-retention stabilization.

## 2. Coherent Decision Formation

Decision formation depends on:

- phase synchronization stability,
- coherence-retention persistence,
- resonance compatibility,
- fluctuation resistance,
- and recursive stabilization reinforcement.

The processor evaluates the qualitative characteristics of coherent phase interaction dynamics rather than isolated state values alone.

## 3. Transitional Decision States

The framework distinguishes transitional coherent decision states from fully stabilized coherent decisions.

Transitional states may contain:

- partially stabilized synchronization structures,
- fluctuating coherence-retention conditions,
- unstable resonance compatibility,
- and dynamically evolving phase-coherence configurations.

These states are treated as dynamically evolving potential coherent decisions rather than meaningless computational noise.

## 4. Recursive Decision Stabilization

Stable coherent decisions recursively reinforce neighboring synchronization structures.

This reinforcement may increase:

- coherent retention persistence,
- synchronization stability,
- resonance-domain compatibility,
- and global coherence consistency.

## 5. Dissipative Decision Collapse

If dissipative fragmentation exceeds coherence accumulation before stabilization completes, coherent decision formation collapses.

This may produce:

- unstable synchronization divergence,
- coherence fragmentation,
- incoherent routing propagation,
- and loss of stable coherent retention.

## 6. Dynamic Stability Condition

Stable coherent decision emergence remains possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

across interacting synchronization structures and coherence-retention domains.

## 7. FPR Interpretation

Within the FPR framework, coherent decision emergence is interpreted as a recursive resonance-driven phase-coherence stabilization process operating through nonlinear synchronization dynamics and distributed coherent-retention interaction.
